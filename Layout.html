Population distribution of Australian capital cities in 2021

As the Australian real estate market continues to evolve, it becomes particularly important to understand its historical trends and current conditions. This visualization aims to shed light on house price changes in Australia, and Melbourne in particular, over the past decade, and the demographic and geographic factors associated with them. By analyzing population distribution, house price trends, house type share and geographic location together, investors, real estate developers and ordinary consumers can make decisions and strategies with greater clarity.

According to the map it can be seen that the state with the highest population is New south Wales followed by Victoria. The majority of the population is located along the south east coast of Australia. The capital cities of these two states are Sydney and Melbourne respectively. Therefore, based on the map of population distribution it is concluded that the demand for housing is higher in the cities of Melbourne and Sydney

Population size distribution and property prices in relation to distance from city center

This scatterplot shows the distribution of population at different distances from Melbourne city center in 2021. As can be seen from the graph, Melbourne city center and its immediate suburbs (within about 50 km) are the most densely populated. As the distance increases, especially after 250km, the population decreases significantly. This suggests that most people tend to live in or near the city center, probably because of the amenities, work and ease of living there. Areas away from city centers, on the other hand, are less populated, possibly because of their remoteness or more limited resources.

This scatterplot shows the distribution of prices for three-bedroom homes at different distances from Melbourne city center in 2021. As can be seen from the graph, prices are relatively high in areas closer to the city center (within about 50 km), especially in the areas closest to the city center. However, when the distance exceeds 50 kilometers, prices start to gradually decline. After 250 kilometers, house prices do not change much and remain at a relatively low level. This may indicate that there is a higher demand for homes in the city center and its immediate suburbs, leading to higher prices, while areas away from the city center, due to remoteness or other factors, have relatively low and stable house prices.

The closer the location is to the city center, the higher the house price and population of three-bedroom apartments. In particular, within 100 kilometers of the city center, both house prices and population show a clear downward trend. In areas over 100km, house prices and population are declining at a relatively slow rate and in some places show a stabilizing or slightly increasing trend. Melbourne city center and its nearby areas attract more people to live there due to its convenience and prosperity, which pushes up house prices. In areas away from the city center, the supply and demand for housing is relatively balanced due to a smaller population, resulting in relatively stable prices.

Analysis of homeownership rates in the continuous birth cohort

The homeownership rate is 67% in 2021, down from 70% in 2006. Beginning in 1971, homeownership rates for younger age groups such as 30-34 and 25-29 declined significantly. At the same time, homeownership rates for earlier birth cohorts, such as 1947-1951, have trended upward over their lifetimes. However, more recent birth cohorts generally have lower homeownership rates compared to birth cohorts from earlier eras. This reflects differences in homeownership between age and birth cohorts. Continuous birth cohorts have consistently lower homeownership rates than older cohorts.

Top 10 crime local government areas near Melbourne city center in 2021

Melbourne City Center had the highest crime rate with a staggering 13,712 incidents, while "Glen Eira" had the lowest with only 5,127 incidents. The rest of the neighborhoods fall in between. For those considering buying or renting a home in Melbourne city center, it's important to consider the safety factor. Additionally, for investors, knowing this data can help them make more informed investment decisions, as choosing safer areas may increase the attractiveness and value of their properties.
